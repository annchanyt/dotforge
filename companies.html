Dotforge Impact 1 - Spring '15

Mobile Power: Building a Grid Without Wires

Now deployed in the Gambia, Mobile Power is a cleantech startup that intends to replace diesel-powered generators with smart rechargeable batteries as a way of life, in developing countries. The batteries are designed to recharge by solar power, and are tracked using cellular technology. Mobile Power’s distribution network comprises of existing village shops, and they work with microfinance institutions on the ground to finance each local rollout in towns and villages.

Genre:CleantechEnergy

Delivrd: Uber for Food

Founded by tech entrepreneur Paul Rawlings (ex-CEO of Screach, now exited), Deliverd delivers healthy lunches to your desk. They use existing commercial and social kitchens to manufacture their on-demand lunches, with a highly sophisticated technical backend which connects kitchens, delivery agents, and customers for a prompt lunchtime service. It is now live in Sheffield and its kitchens include the Cathedral Archer project, a homeless shelter which has a professional kitchen trained to produce quality food.

Genre:FoodOn-Demand MarketplaceLogistics

OnShowcase: Buffer for Schools

Schools are adapting the way they communicate with parents and guardians. Many are now using online resources such as blogs and social media, but these are time consuming activities and difficult to maintain. onShowcase is a platform where every staff member in the school can snap photos of school life, in the “send and forget” mind-set, with the reassurance that their photo won’t be accessible publicly until it has been reviewed by a senior team member. onShowcase empowers the staff with freedom and creativity, whilst also ensuring the school is compliant with all privacy policies when the photos are pushed out to other online resources, such as social media.

Genre:EdTech

Care Selector: Moneysupermarket for Care

The need for elderly care is a large and growing market in the UK. Nevertheless it is difficult to compare across the market and find the best care home for your needs. Furthermore, there is no provider that streamlines the registration of the patient to the care home. Care Selec- tor aims to make this whole process easier: from find- ing the right care home for your needs, to booking and registering. The first rollout has been throughout Bristol where they have signed up 13% of all care homes and serviced 60 enquiries of which 15% have already placed an individual in a home.

Genre:GeriatricE-Commerce

CoverVideo: Video Recruitment Platform

CoverVideo enables employers to recruit through the medium of video. This adds another dimension to the candidate’s application, and in situations where writing is a problem (such as a candidate suffering from dyslexia), video recruitment gives the opportunity for the candidate’s skills and personality to shine through. This is particularly helpful for sales and customer-facing roles.

Genre:Recruitment

Learner Lane: Match.com for Tutoring

LearnerLane is an intelligent marketplace that matches the best tutor to the student or child. Through a short questionnaire, students and parents can tailor their ask, while the platform provides a booking, contracting and payments process, making it simpler for students and parents to manage their relationship with tutors. The team is currently working with Sheffield Futures, which operates the largest network of support for schools and young people in Sheffield, testing the proposition and engagement with parents and teachers.

Genre:EdTechE-Commerce

Open Cinema: AirBnB for Cinema

Open Cinema allows any community space—an office, a care home, a pub—to become a fully licensed cinema and sell its own tickets. With 10 venues across the UK up and running, this company aims to disrupt film ex- hibition through enabling very local film communities to manage themselves and the movies they next watch together. Open Cinema was originally launched in a homeless shelter in Soho square to bring its members together through sharing the experience of watching a movie.

Genre:MediaSharing Economy

StrokeCare: Telehealth for Stroke Patients

Stroke usually affects the mobility of the patient. This makes it hard for the patient and often their carers to seek professional support or receive consistent medical help. StrokeCare is creating a solution which includes local support networks, rehabilitation information and video content and a telehealth platform to connect stroke patients with professionals through video chat- ting.

Genre:Health

PepVend: Smart Vending Machines

PepVend is creating smart vending machines built on a cloud infrastructure to improve the logistical challenge of replenishing and servicing office based vending. The target market of the product is large and small office spaces. The company is revenue generating with good penetration with office spaces and businesses in the Sheffield area.

Genre:LogisticsInternet of Things

Polen: Giving through Affiliate Marketing

Polen enables users to donate to charities through perform- ing affiliate actions, such as watching an advertiser’s video, or signing up to a product. It is a new way for brands to demonstrate their wider social responsibility, while building relationships with their customers and directly supporting charities and causes.

Genre:EdTech

Chummunity: The Marketplace for Writers

Chummunity unites writers with people who have a story to tell, creating a physical book that becomes a legacy or memento. The process of talking and being able to tell one’s story can be curative for some. This has potential application in mental health and care for the terminally ill.

Genre:E-Commerce

PiP (Pay-in-Person): Make Online Purchases with Offline Payments

Featuring experienced payments veteran, Richard Beaton, on its team, PiP is a payment method for users to make cash payments for online purchases. The company has contracted with the UK and Irish post offices as payment points, and is midway through discussions with established e-commerce payment platforms to be an additional payment option made available to customers at the point of purchase.

Genre:Payments

Logsit

Logsit is an app that records any unscheduled, recurring events. This includes a huge subset of human behaviour, from taking medication to going for a run to watching a film. Using the app is simple: you add the actions you want to keep track of and swipe to check-in each time you do them. Logsit automatically stamps the time and location and users can add a note and photo if they like.

Genre:Utility

HIVE

We want HIVE to be the go to instant collaboration tool for designers and developers. HIVE’s philosophy is to be seamless, almost invisible – essentially allowing designers/developers to share files, annotate documents and communicate with each other with just one click.

Genre:Project Management

Coacher

Hashtrack

Agencies and brands currently have no way to determine both the effectiveness and ROI of current hashtag conversations, they are merely taking a stab in the dark. Posts with hashtags get on average 2x as much engagement as those without. 2x more engagement means 2x more reach and potentially 2x as many customers. Hashtrack provides in depth and actionable insights relating to the conversation around a specific hashtag over the social media landscape.

Genre:MediaData Analysis

CarMatchin

CarMatchin helps consumers start their car buying experience, removing the barriers to entering the new car market, such as industry jargon, poor showroom experiences at dealerships and a lack of trust in car sales industry professionals. The platform captures lifestyle needs exactly the same way consumers describe them, recommends suitable cars and supports the user onto the next stage of the car buying journey by setting up and booking test drives with local dealers.

Genre:Ecommerce

Twile

Twile is planning to disrupt the online family history market - which is worth $2bn globally - by making it more exciting and engaging for the wider family, especially the younger generation. Twile allows family historians to import their family trees and automatically create a rich, visual timelines of their ancestry made up of milestones, photos and videos, which their family can explore and contribute to. While online services like Ancestry allow family historians to research their ancestry and store their findings, they offer little to help them share this with their wider family in an engaging way.

Genre:MediaFamily
